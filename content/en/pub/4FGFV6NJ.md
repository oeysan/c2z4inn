    <h1>About</h1>
    Urban-Andreas and Mølstad explore how schools use comparisons and quantifications, like test scores and rankings, to market themselves. They examine how these practices influence various levels of the educational system, from policy-making to classroom teaching. Their research is important because it helps us understand the impact of these numbers on education quality and decision-making. By analyzing how schools present themselves through data, the authors provide insights that can help educators, policymakers, and students make more informed choices. This study is particularly useful for those involved in comparative education, curriculum research, and teacher education, offering practical guidance on conducting research in these areas.
  </article>

    <h1>Scientific abstract</h1>
    "Numbers and Knowledge in Education discusses contemporary trends and activities related to comparisons and quantifications. It aims to help scholars to conduct empirically based research on how comparisons and quantifications are instituted in practice at different levels in the educational system. Numbers and Knowledge in Education will be of great interest to academics, researchers and post-graduate students in the fields of comparative education, curriculum research and policy studies. It will also appeal to those in the fields of teacher education, including student teachers"
